Parkinson’s Disease In Nepal


Introduction:

 

Parkinson’s Disease (PD) is a neurological disorder that affects millions worldwide, yet in countries like Nepal, its understanding and management remain challenging. With a lack of awareness, limited healthcare infrastructure, and cultural stigmas surrounding neurological disorders, individuals living with PD in Nepal often face immense hurdles. This blog post aims to shed light on the status of Parkinson’s Disease in Nepal, exploring its nuances, challenges, and avenues for improvement.

Understanding Parkinson’s Disease:

 

Parkinson’s Disease is characterized by the progressive degeneration of dopamine-producing cells in the brain, leading to symptoms such as tremors, stiffness, slowness of movement, and balance difficulties. While the exact cause remains unknown, both genetic predisposition and environmental factors are believed to contribute to its development.

Symptoms:

Parkinson’s symptoms may include:

  • Rhythmic shaking, called tremor, usually begins in a limb, often your hand or fingers. You may rub your thumb and forefinger back and forth. This is known as a pill-rolling tremor. Your hand may tremble when it’s at rest. The shaking may decrease when you are performing tasks.
  • Slowed movement, known as bradykinesia.Over time, Parkinson’s disease may slow your movement, making simple tasks difficult and time-consuming. Your steps may become shorter when you walk. It may be difficult to get out of a chair. You may drag or shuffle your feet as you try to walk.
  • Rigid muscles.Muscle stiffness may occur in any part of your body. The stiff muscles can be painful and limit your range of motion.
  • Impaired posture and balance.Your posture may become stooped. Or you may fall or have balance problems as a result of Parkinson’s disease.
  • Loss of automatic movements.You may have a decreased ability to perform unconscious movements, including blinking, smiling or swinging your arms when you walk.
  • Speech changes.You may speak softly or quickly, slur, or hesitate before talking. Your speech may be more of a monotone rather than have the usual speech patterns.
  • Writing changes.It may become hard to write, and your writing may appear small.
